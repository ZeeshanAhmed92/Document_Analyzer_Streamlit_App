2. Executive Summary

2.1 Sampling Methodology

Please note that the audit was conducted based on a sampling approach, meaning that findings and conclusions are based on a selected sample of processes and data, not on 100% evaluation. The goal is to provide reasonable assurance rather than absolute certainty. This methodology proved effective and enabled the organization to identify targeted improvement actions.

2.2 General Impressions of the Management System

The ISMS at Vlectra demonstrates a strong commitment to information security, with comprehensive policies and procedures aligned to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 requirements. Most controls are well implemented and supported by documented evidence, reflecting mature organizational and technological security practices.

2.2.1 Highlights

  • ✔ Comprehensive Information Security Policy covering scope, responsibilities, enforcement, and management commitment.
  • ✔ Well-defined access control, identity management, and authentication policies with supporting evidence.
  • ✔ Effective incident management procedures including monitoring, reporting, and response coordination.
  • ✔ Strong supplier relationship management with contracts including security clauses and risk assessments.
  • ✔ Robust backup and restoration policies with documented testing and off-site storage.
  • ✔ Active security awareness, training, and disciplinary processes ensuring compliance.
  • ✔ Secure physical access controls and media disposal policies in place.
  • ✔ Documented secure development lifecycle and application security testing.

2.2.2 Findings

While the ISMS is largely compliant, some areas require further documentation or explicit evidence to fully demonstrate compliance and control effectiveness. These include:

  • Information labelling guidelines and evidence of their application are not explicitly documented.
  • Cloud services security controls and approval processes lack explicit documented evidence.
  • Intellectual property rights management and software licensing procedures need formal documentation.
  • Records protection, secure disposal, and backup media policies require clearer evidence.
  • Remote working and mobile device protection policies are not explicitly documented.
  • Cabling security controls per local or European regulations are not evidenced.
  • Physical security monitoring lacks explicit CCTV or continuous monitoring documentation.
  • Environmental threat protection controls (fire, flood, power failure) are insufficiently detailed.
  • Equipment siting and protection from environmental hazards need formal policies.
  • Technical redundancy controls for information processing facilities are not explicitly documented.
  • Protection of information systems during audit testing is not clearly addressed.
  • Equipment maintenance policy is limited and requires comprehensive coverage.

2.2.3 Non-conformities identified

  • ✘ Lack of documented information labelling procedures and evidence.
  • ✘ Missing explicit cloud services security controls and approval evidence.
  • ✘ No formal documentation or evidence for intellectual property rights compliance.
  • ✘ Insufficient documented evidence for records protection and secure disposal.
  • ✘ Absence of documented remote working and mobile device protection policies.
  • ✘ No documented cabling security controls per regulations.
  • ✘ Physical security monitoring lacks CCTV or continuous monitoring documentation.
  • ✘ Environmental threat protection controls are not sufficiently documented.
  • ✘ Equipment siting and environmental protection policies are missing.
  • ✘ No explicit technical redundancy controls documented for critical facilities.
  • ✘ Lack of documented controls for protection during audit testing.
  • ✘ Equipment maintenance policy coverage is limited and lacks detail.

2.2.4 Opportunities for improvement

  • Develop and document comprehensive information labelling guidelines and provide evidence of their application.
  • Establish explicit cloud services security policies including approval workflows and controls, supported by evidence.
  • Formalize intellectual property rights management and software licensing procedures with documented evidence.
  • Enhance records protection and secure disposal policies with clear documentation and evidence.
  • Document remote working and mobile device protection policies, including controls and monitoring.
  • Implement and document cabling security controls aligned with local and European regulations.
  • Introduce physical security monitoring measures such as CCTV and intrusion detection, and document their operation.
  • Develop detailed controls and procedures for protection against physical and environmental threats.
  • Establish equipment siting and environmental protection policies to mitigate physical risks.
  • Document and implement technical redundancy controls for critical information processing facilities.
  • Create specific procedures to protect information systems during audit testing, including access restrictions and monitoring.
  • Expand equipment maintenance policies to cover all IT assets with schedules, responsibilities, and records.

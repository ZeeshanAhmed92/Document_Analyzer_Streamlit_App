2. Executive Summary

2.1 Sampling Methodology

Please note that the audit was conducted based on a sampling approach, meaning that findings and conclusions are based on a selected sample of processes and data, not on 100% evaluation. The goal is to provide reasonable assurance rather than absolute certainty. This methodology proved effective and enabled the organization to identify targeted improvement actions.

2.2 General Impressions of the Management System

The ISMS at Vlectra demonstrates a strong commitment to information security aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 requirements. Most controls are well documented and implemented, with comprehensive policies covering organizational, people, physical, and technological controls. Management involvement and regular reviews are evident, supporting continuous improvement.

2.2.1 Highlights

  • ✔ Comprehensive Information Security Policy with clear roles and responsibilities.
  • ✔ Effective access control and identity management processes including two-factor authentication.
  • ✔ Robust incident management procedures with documented response and learning mechanisms.
  • ✔ Well-maintained asset inventory and classification schemes.
  • ✔ Strong network security with firewalls, segregation, and monitoring.
  • ✔ Documented secure development lifecycle and application security testing.
  • ✔ Regular internal and external audits supporting compliance and continual improvement.

2.2.2 Findings

While the ISMS is largely compliant, some areas require further documentation or formalization to strengthen controls and ensure full alignment with ISO 27001:2022:

  • Information classification and information transfer controls need explicit documented procedures.
  • Physical security perimeter and entry controls lack explicit documented evidence.
  • Cloud services security controls require formal documentation including approvals and TLS testing.
  • Contact procedures with authorities and special interest groups should be explicitly documented.
  • Data leakage prevention lacks explicit technical controls and monitoring tools.
  • Redundancy of information processing facilities and protection during audit testing need formal documented controls.
  • Remote working policy and confidentiality/non-disclosure agreements require formal documentation.
  • Capacity management and equipment maintenance policies need further formalization and documentation.

2.2.3 Non-conformities identified

  • ✘ No explicit documented physical security perimeter controls such as card swipe, alarms, or secured racks.
  • ✘ Lack of documented NDA requirements and approvals for external providers and employees.
  • ✘ Missing documented cloud service security controls including approval and TLS testing.
  • ✘ No documented procedures for contact with authorities.
  • ✘ No explicit documented controls for protection of information systems during audit and penetration testing.
  • ✘ Lack of documented physical entry controls and secure area visitor escort procedures.
  • ✘ No documented controls for physical and environmental threat protection.
  • ✘ Missing documented cabling security and supporting utilities management.

2.2.4 Opportunities for improvement

  • Develop and document explicit information classification and transfer procedures to ensure consistent handling and protection of data.
  • Formalize and document physical security perimeter and entry controls including visitor management and monitoring systems such as CCTV.
  • Establish and document cloud services security policies covering approvals, access restrictions, and TLS security testing.
  • Implement documented procedures for contact with authorities and maintain formal relationships with special interest groups and security forums.
  • Introduce technical data leakage prevention (DLP) tools and monitoring to enhance data protection.
  • Document redundancy and failover mechanisms for critical information processing facilities to improve availability.
  • Define and document procedures to protect information systems during audit and penetration testing activities.
  • Formalize remote working policies including device management, access controls, and security awareness.
  • Develop comprehensive equipment maintenance policies covering all IT and physical assets with schedules and responsibilities.
  • Document capacity management processes including monitoring, forecasting, and resource planning.
  • Establish and document confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement requirements and approval processes for all relevant parties.
